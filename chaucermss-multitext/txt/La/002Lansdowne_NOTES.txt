Transcription notes for LansdowneCapitalization.1)  Although many of his "m"s have the form which we associate withcapitalization, these are so numerous that we decided to level the majority to lower-case on the assumption that this letter form is interchangeable (as with "w").  The only times we have capitalized "m"is at the beginning of verse lines, the name "Mellibee" and instanceswhere its size appeared to be large.2)  The letter "n" occasionally takes upon its upper-case form wherethe form does not seem necessary, such as for the word "No" in themiddle of a verse line.  Since these occurances are rare, we have observed the capitalization.3)  His lower-case "a"s always have a single chamber, so two-chambered"a"s have been capitalized, especially in the prose.4)  Sometimes letters at the beginning of a prose line are given their upper-case form, even though it is the middle of a sentence (perhaps forgetting that he is writing prose and not verse?).  We have acknowledgedthese.Punctuation.1)  We have omitted the vertical strokes which appear to proliferatethis ms, in agreement with the WBP transcription.2)  We have observed punctuation marks, feeling that these do serveas a means of punctuation in the majority of cases, perhaps in the roleof a virgule.3)  We have observed semi-colons, especially in the prose.  However,where a semi-colon has been partially over written by a paraph, we havenot recorded it.  However, this seems to be a topic which we feelshould be open for discussion.  You may wish to revise this policy.4)  Lansdowne does not appear to be especially concerned with justifyinghis margins in the prose.  He does not appear to have used fillers.  However, sometimes he uses hyphenation where none is necessary, in which case we have recorded these, even at line ends where they mightbe mistaken for fillers.5)  The scribe sometimes uses a "=" mark in his prose, perhaps to indicatethe inclusion of a paraph.  We have been unsure how to represent this.  Our decisions have varied.  This needs some discussion and standardization.          *********
